## Design Context

### Users

Perseval is for engineers building and operating tool-using and multi-agent
systems. They use it locally during debugging and remediation: scan many runs,
find recurring behavior failures, inspect the exact span evidence, compare
execution shapes, and turn failures into reviewed evals. They are comfortable
with dense developer tools and expect keyboard-speed interactions.

### Brand Personality

Fast, dense, forensic. Perseval should feel calm under pressure and precise
about evidence. It should communicate that every visual claim can be traced
back to a concrete run, span, or finding.

### Aesthetic Direction

A dark, industrial trace workbench with the information density and interaction
clarity of AgentPrism and Raindrop Workshop, expressed as a native macOS tool
rather than a browser dashboard. The timeline is the memorable center of the
product: agent, model, and tool work read as an execution score with restrained
categorical color. Avoid generic AI gradients, glass panels, oversized metrics,
rounded-card dashboards, and decorative monospace.

### Design Principles

1. Evidence before ornament: selection, color, and emphasis must identify real
   trace structure or failure evidence.
2. Trace-first hierarchy: the execution timeline and tree dominate; metadata
   and payloads support them without stealing focus.
3. Dense but legible: use compact native controls, strong alignment, and
   progressive disclosure rather than hiding important information.
4. One interaction model: selecting a run or span synchronizes the tree,
   timeline, and inspector.
5. Local-native speed: keyboard navigation, immediate filtering, predictable
   focus, and no network dependency for the demo path.
